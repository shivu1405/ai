SYS-01 LinkedIn Founder Content Engine [ CLICK TO EXPAND ]

Problem: Founders know they should post consistently but face a blank page every week — and generic posts get ignored.

Outcomes

  • 10 ready-to-post drafts in under 30 minutes
  • Consistent founder voice across every post
  • Story-driven format built for real engagement
  • Never sounds like generic AI output
Why it works: Structured constraints force specificity. The system prevents AI defaulting to vague language — every post comes out with a real story and a real point.

Example Prompt

ROLE: You are a startup founder writing authentic LinkedIn content. TASK: Write a post about [FOUNDER EXPERIENCE/LESSON]. FORMAT: Line 1: Hook — stops the scroll Lines 2–5: Specific story from building your company Lines 6–8: Core lesson, stated simply and directly Lines 9–10: One actionable takeaway Line 11: Question that invites reply CONSTRAINTS: - Tone: conversational, honest - Length: 130–160 words - Max 3 hashtags, relevant only - Never start with "I" or "Excited to share" INPUT: [Paste your experience here]
SYS-02 Competitor Research Automation [ CLICK TO EXPAND ]

Problem: Founders spend 3–5 hours on competitor research that ends up scattered and never organized into anything useful.

Outcomes

  • Structured competitor analysis in under 20 minutes
  • Consistent format for every company researched
  • Strategic insights, not just surface-level facts
  • Gap analysis tailored to your startup's position
Why it works: Giving AI your startup context forces it to frame insights relative to your actual situation. The gap section turns raw research into real strategy.

Example Prompt

ROLE: You are a startup market intelligence analyst. TASK: Analyze [COMPETITOR NAME] as a competitor to my startup. PROVIDE (with headers): 1. Core Product — what they sell and who it's for 2. Pricing Model — tiers, billing 3. Key Differentiators — their claimed strengths 4. Weaknesses — real complaints from G2/Reddit 5. ICP — their ideal customer 6. Gap Opportunity — where I could win against them MY STARTUP: [2–3 sentences about your product] OUTPUT: Use structured tables where possible.
SYS-03 SaaS Landing Page Copy Generator [ CLICK TO EXPAND ]

Problem: Founders spend weeks on copy or pay agencies thousands — and still get messaging that doesn't convert.

Outcomes

  • Full landing page copy draft in one session
  • 3 headline variants ready for A/B testing
  • Feature copy that sells outcomes, not features
  • CTA options that go beyond "Get Started"
Why it works: The product brief forces founders to clarify positioning first. Feature → benefit framing ensures copy sells outcomes — the #1 mistake SaaS pages make.

Example Prompt

ROLE: You are a direct-response SaaS copywriter. PRODUCT BRIEF: Name: [PRODUCT NAME] One-liner: [What it does] Target Customer: [Persona] Top 3 Pain Points: [List them] Key Features: [List top 3] Main Competitor: [Name] DELIVERABLES: H1: 3 headlines (under 10 words, outcome-focused) Subheadline: Expands H1, 1–2 lines Problem Block: 2–3 sentences Feature → Benefit: "So you can..." CTA: 3 button copy options (not "Get Started") TONE: Clear, confident. Write for a skeptic.
